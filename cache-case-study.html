Project Overview

Challenges

Many people have financial goals that seem too hard or impossible to reach, but those same people might not realize that what they casually spend in a day, week or month could be what's holding them back. Saving for a vacation could mean cutting out that over-priced salad you have for lunch twice a week or ending a subscription you don't use anymore but just never got around to cancelling. If you were told your vacation goal meant you had to save $2.36 a day, you might realize your morning coffee is standing in the way of your dream trip.

Cache is an application that helps the average person save money for a large expenditure. Objective

  • To help people save money quickly in preparation for a big purchase or expenditure.
  • Cache will give people a safe, careful and stress free place to passively save their money

My role

  • UI design (visual design, usability testing and prototyping)

Tools used

  • Adobe XD, Illustrator and Photoshop

Design Process


Ideate

Building the brand

To begin the design process, there needed to be an established brand identity. I was tasked with creating the brand from scratch. That process included establishing the product name, logo and icon, color palette, imagery guidelines and typography.

Square one was the name. I originally searched for a suitable animal mascot. I was looking for an animal that stockpiles or hoards its belongings. That search came back with squirrels, hamsters and a few birds, which wasn't the inspiration I was looking for. The word "caching" kept popping up when describing these animals and suddenly it clicked: cache, meaning to store away in hiding or for future use.

The word cache seems to be associated with pirates and treasure. So I wanted to loosely conform to that association with the application icon by making it a gold coin.

User flows

With the overall brand guidelines in mind, I was off to the wireframing phase. The research showed that users are tech savvy but don't have much interest in spending time on their finances. They want to input their values, set up a plan and occasionally check back in to be sure they're still on the right track. The process needs to be quick and painless.

Wireframing

With the user flows in place, it was time to do some quick sketching to begin building the framework for this product.

The mid-fidelity wireframes needed to have enough detail to begin prototyping a usability test.

Prototyping and User Testing

User testing

I created a simple prototype from the mid-fidelity wireframes to send off to potential users/testers. I recruited 5 people with differing experience in using financial applications.

User testing revealed:

  • "Auto pay" was confusing to users. The question "what is auto pay?" came up a couple times.
  • Some of the buttons didn't make sense. Ex: "Finish" on the Cache page.
  • There needs to be an "add more" type button when entering income in the income fields along with expenses.
  • There should be more excitement when telling users that they're doing a good job.
  • The users didn't seem to recognize when they had completed the set up process.

Present

Creating a Cache

Setting up a Cache is a simple process. The user can set up as many as they'd like at once. With the auto-save feature linked to their financial institution, setting up a savings plan is just a few clicks away.

Reviewing what you're spending and where

Users want to easily see the progress they've been making.

The app will offer a quick way to sort through transactions that don't appear to match a category to keep everything organized for the user.

On-the-go or at home

When a user is out and about they might not be keeping up with their finances. Cache is made to be used on the go so the user can be on top of their savings at all times, but sometimes it's nice to have a larger screen present, especially when you have made a lot of different purchases or want to reevaluate your budget. That is where the Cache desktop application comes in. You have access to all of the same great features from the comfort of your workspace
