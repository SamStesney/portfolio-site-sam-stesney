The world's most accessible middleman for photographers and models to find each other

UI/UX Design

Sam Stesney

Platform

Responsive web app

The Idea

Giving users the ability to interact with models or photographers will elevate their skills in their respective role and promote a friendly, more cost-efficient environment for those who want to break into the scene. This product will be a driving factor behind more and more high-quality content for users to share or use on a professional level. The location-based features will save the user a great deal of time and create a productive process time and time again.

How did I get here?

After conducting market research and user interviews I found that the market was lacking a simple and intuitive way for hobby/amateur photographers and models to reach out to one another through a service dedicated to helping them thrive. Any app that has images as a main focus and the ability to interact with each other has folks reaching out to each other, but through user interviews I was able to deduce that those methods are inefficient and sometimes viewed as intrusive or creepy. The idea of giving people a platform to reach out to people who want to be reached out to was born.

Who are we talking about?

User-centric design

It's impossible to say a design is centered around the user if the user is never involved. To stay true to the style of user-centric design, I requested the help of average users very early on. After conducting interviews to find gaps in the market, I received great insight on what the average person might be looking for in an app like LookBook. I quickly learned that it's very important for everyone to feel safe while using the app, so I created the reviews and recommendation system to help vet photographers and models. People want to be able to see the work of the users they're hoping to link up with, so I added a feed to every user's profile to give them a chance to showcase their best work. Lastly, flexible scheduling is important to people from all walks of life, so I created a booking feature to give everyone a chance to plan ahead or head out for a photoshoot on the fly

With an established user flow I was able to sketch the screens and apply rapid prototyping for users to have a real visual to test out. These quick sketches were used to create a very low-fidelity prototype which allowed me to conduct more in-person user testing to receive valuable feedback about the core user experience and how the UI would potentially work going forward.

The early stages of UI

With all of the valuable feedback from the users I brought in early in the process, I was able to start creating with minimal fear of my design being rejected by users in the long run. The mid-fidelity wireframes were born.

Getting moody

The foundation is set, the frame is in place, time to start decorating.

LookBook is best used on-the-go. To create an easy on-the-go app it needs to be simple. Powerful, but simple. With that in mind, I knew it also needed to be sleek and easy to look at. I wanted to give it a sense of class and simplicity at the same time. It needed high contrast and an accent color that would draw the eye. It was time for a mood board

Setting the standard

Knowing how I wanted the app to make the user feel was the first step in the "decoration" process. It was time to establish exactly how I wanted everything to look in the end with a style guide.

Piecing it all together

With all of the pieces of the puzzle in hand, it's time to place them and finally get a good look at the final image.
